Serve With Us

Give a year. Change a life — including your own.

Lay missionaries from around the world come to Trujillo to live, serve, and grow alongside the children of Finca del Niño.

Missioner Program

Lay Missionary Service — One year in Honduras

Lay missionaries commit to a one-year term of service at Finca del Niño, living in community with the children, learning Spanish, and serving in roles ranging from house parent to teacher to healthcare aide.

Who can apply?

Any adult (18+) with a servant heart, openness to faith, and commitment to a full year of service. No specific skills required — just a willingness to show up every day for the children.

What will I do?

House parent duties, tutoring, clinic support, farm work, community outreach, and — most importantly — being present for the children each day.

What is provided?

Housing, meals, health insurance, Spanish training, and a stipend. You provide your own flights and fundraise your living support.

Immersion Trips

Come and see — short-term immersion trips

Not ready for a full year? Immersion trips let you experience the Finca firsthand — spending a week or two in Trujillo, serving alongside the children and missionaries.

What to expect on an immersion trip

  • ✓Live and eat on the Finca alongside the children and missionaries
  • ✓Participate in daily routines — school, farm work, prayer, meals
  • ✓Help with construction, gardening, tutoring, or healthcare
  • ✓Visit the local Garífuna community in Trujillo
  • ✓Trips typically run 1–2 weeks; group or individual

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak Spanish? +

Basic Spanish is helpful but not required for application. Spanish training is provided as part of the missionary program.

Is this only for Catholics? +

The Finca is rooted in the Catholic faith and missionaries participate in faith life, but people of all backgrounds who respect the Catholic mission are welcome to apply.

What is the age range for missionaries? +

Most missionaries are in their 20s–40s, but the program is open to adults of all ages who meet the physical and commitment requirements.

How is safety managed in Honduras? +

The Finca has operated safely in Trujillo for over 30 years. Missionaries are oriented to local safety protocols and are never left without support. Contact us for a detailed safety briefing.
