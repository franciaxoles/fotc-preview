What We Do

A full life — every day at the Finca

Education, healthcare, spiritual formation, and family — all under one roof in Trujillo, Honduras.

Our Focus

Whole-child care — body, mind, and spirit

The Finca provides everything a child needs to thrive. Not just survival — but flourishing.

🏡

A Loving Home

Staffed by lay missionaries serving as house parents. Each child has a room, a family, and people who show up every day.

🏫

Education

Uniforms, materials, and school fees covered. The Finca also provides tutoring and learning support for every child.

🏥

Healthcare

An on-site clinic serves the children and the broader Trujillo community. Preventive care, checkups, and emergency treatment — all on the Finca.

✝️

Spiritual Formation

Faith is at the heart of the Finca. Children participate in Mass, prayer, and community celebrations that connect them to their culture and their God.

🍽️

Nutrition

Three nutritious meals per day, grown and prepared on the Finca. The farm produces vegetables, fruit, and protein for the family table.

🌿

Community Outreach

Beyond the 20 resident children, the Finca serves hundreds of community members through its school, clinic, and vocational programs.

A Typical Day

Morning to night — a day at the Finca

6:00 AM
Morning prayer & breakfast
The day begins together — with prayer, a home-cooked breakfast, and the energy of a house full of children.
7:30 AM
School
Children attend school either on the Finca or in town. The Finca covers all fees, uniforms, and materials.
12:00 PM
Lunch & afternoon free time
Soccer on the campo, swimming, art, and climbing every tree they can find.
3:00 PM
Homework & tutoring
Missionaries help with homework and provide extra tutoring for children who need support.
6:00 PM
Dinner & evening prayer
The family gathers for dinner, stories, and evening prayer — ending the day as it began: together.
Community Impact

Beyond the Finca — Trujillo's Garífuna coast

The Finca's impact extends far beyond its 20 resident children. The clinic, school, and vocational programs serve hundreds of families in the surrounding Garífuna community each year.

Trujillo, Colón — Honduras's forgotten coast

Trujillo sits on the north coast of Honduras, home to the Garífuna people — an Afro-Indigenous community with a rich culture that faces deep poverty, limited healthcare access, and high rates of family displacement.

The Finca is a beacon in this community — offering free healthcare at its clinic, educational support, and a model of hope rooted in faith and dignity.
