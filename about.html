Our Story

30 years of love on the Garífuna coast

Farm of the Child began as a calling — and became a home for generations of children in Trujillo, Honduras.

Our Story

A mission rooted in faith and family

Over 30 years ago, Vincent and Zulena Pescatore answered a call to serve vulnerable children on the north coast of Honduras. What began as a small mission in Trujillo — a city on Honduras's Garífuna coast — grew into Finca del Niño: a residential family home where children find belonging, healing, and hope.

The Finca is not an orphanage. It is a family — structured around the model of the Holy Family, where children live with house parents, attend school, receive healthcare at the on-site clinic, and grow in faith alongside lay missionaries who give a year or more of their lives to serve.

Today, 20 children call the Finca home. Hundreds more in the surrounding community receive support through the Finca's school and outreach programs.

Founders

Vincent & Zulena Pescatore

For over three decades, Vincent and Zulena have dedicated their lives to the children of Trujillo — building not just a home, but a community rooted in faith, love, and dignity.

"Our children are not charity cases — they are gifts. Every one of them carries a light that the world needs to see."

Vincent and Zulena's mission was inspired by their Catholic faith and a deep conviction that every vulnerable child deserves a true family. Over 30 years in Trujillo, they have built a home, a school, a clinic, and a community of missionaries who carry their vision forward.

Our Team

Lay missionaries living the mission

The Finca is staffed by dedicated lay missionaries — men and women who give one or more years of their lives to serve as house parents, teachers, healthcare workers, and community builders in Trujillo.

Our Honduras team consists of lay missionaries from the U.S. and around the world who commit to one-year terms of service, living alongside the children, learning Spanish, and becoming part of the Trujillo community.
